Analytical Report: Conference Publications (Interactive)

Introduction: This report examines a corpus of conference papers through multiple analytical lenses: thematic keyword trends, sentiment in abstracts, predictive modeling of citation impact, anomaly detection in impact metrics, supervised classification of paper types, and temporal forecasting of download activity. The analyses are intended to provide a comprehensive, data-driven view of research evolution and impact measurement.

1. Keyword & Sentiment Trend Analysis

Keyword & Sentiment Analysis: We focus on the top five recurring keywords (, visual analytics, visualization, interaction, information visualization) to trace thematic trajectories across years. By coupling keyword frequency with average abstract sentiment, we can infer whether emergent topics are discussed in more positive, neutral, or negative tones, which may correlate with community reception or research maturity. The interactive plot allows zooming into particular windows of years and comparing trends side-by-side.

2. Predictive Modeling of Citation Count

Predictive Modeling of Citation Count: Using textual features from abstracts and categorical metadata, a Random Forest regressor estimates paper citation counts. The scatter plot contrasts actual versus predicted citation counts, with point coloring indicating prediction error. Model performance (reported here as MAE=29.32 and RÂ²=0.06) provides a benchmark for model reliability and indicates room for feature engineering (e.g., network, temporal features) to improve accuracy.

3. Anomaly Detection in Citations & Downloads

Anomaly Detection in Impact Metrics: An Isolation Forest isolates unusual combinations of citation and download counts that deviate from typical patterns. Such anomalies may point to highly influential papers, data artifacts, or external events (e.g., press coverage). Analysts can inspect anomalous points to determine if the anomaly reflects genuine research influence or reporting/collection errors.

4. Text Classification: PaperType Prediction

PaperType Classification: A logistic regression model trained on textual features predicts the type of paper (e.g., full paper, short paper, demo). The confusion matrix summarizes classification behavior and, with an observed accuracy of 0.76, suggests baseline separability of types via textual cues. Misclassifications indicate overlapping writing styles or inconsistent labeling that merit further annotation or richer features.

5. Temporal Analysis & Forecast of Downloads

Temporal Forecasting of Downloads: We compute the historical mean downloads per year and apply a simple ARIMA-based forecast to project average downloads for the next five years. This provides a high-level view of readership trends and can aid program committees or publishers in assessing long-term engagement. Confidence intervals communicate forecast uncertainty.

Conclusion

Conclusion: The multimodal analyses presented collectively advance understanding of thematic prominence, sentiment, predictive signals of impact, outlier behavior, and temporal engagement dynamics in conference publications. Future work may incorporate co-authorship networks, citation graph features, richer semantic embeddings (e.g., BERT), and causal inference methods to strengthen interpretability and predictive performance.
